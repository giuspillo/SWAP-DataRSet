Quiet Days in Clichy is a novella written by Henry Miller. It is based on his experience as Parisian expatriate in the early 1930s, when he and Alfred Perlès, both struggling writers, shared a small apartment in suburban Clichy (at 4 Avenue Anatole-France). It takes place around the time Miller was writing Black Spring. According to his photographer friend George Brassaï, Miller admitted the title is “completely misleading. ”
