The Animal Family is a 1965 children's novel by American poet and critic Randall Jarrell, illustrated by noted children's book illustrator Maurice Sendak. It is a 1966 Newbery Honor book and has a significant following among adult readers.
Plot summary
A man, a boy, a mermaid, a lynx and a bear - all orphans - find a home together in a log cabin in the woods by the sea. Through their shared experiences and self-created myth of their own origins, they create their own unique identities.
