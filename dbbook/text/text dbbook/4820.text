Chrysanthemum is the title of a 1991 children's picture book by the American writer and illustrator Kevin Henkes. The plot deals with a young mouse named Chrysanthemum, who loves her name until classmates tease her about it. However, a pregnant teacher (called Delphinium) defends her and her family comfort her. At the end of the story, the teacher gives birth to a girl and decides to name her Chrysanthemum too. Chrysanthemum was chosen as an ALA Notable Book and the School Library Journal Best Book of the Year, and it is on the Horn Book Fanfare Honor List. Based on a 2007 online poll, the National Education Association named the book one of its "Teachers' Top 100 Books for Children. Summary
The plot deals with a young mouse named Chrysanthemum , who loves her long name until a girl name Victoria and her friends tease her about it. However, a pregnant music teacher (called Delphinium ) defends her saying how a lot of people have long names. and her family comforts her. Adaptions
In 1998 Weston Woods developed an animated story based on the book's illustrations, which was narrated by Meryl Streep . In 2002 it was produced by Newvideo and Scholastic and bundled with 5 other animated stories on a "Scholastic Storybook Treasures" DVD. [5]
