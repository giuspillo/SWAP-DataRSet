Again, Dangerous Visions is the sequel to the science fiction short story anthology Dangerous Visions, first published in 1972. It was edited by Harlan Ellison and illustrated by Ed Emshwiller. Like its predecessor, Again, Dangerous Visions and the stories within it received many awards. The Word for World is Forest, by Ursula K. Le Guin, won a Hugo for Best Novella. "When It Changed" by Joanna Russ won a Nebula Award for Best Short Story. For a second time, Harlan Ellison received a special Hugo for editing the anthology. Again, Dangerous Visions was also published in a two-volume paperback edition by Signet in the US and Pan in the UK. It was to be followed by a third anthology, The Last Dangerous Visions, but this was never published.
